From Bucharest Stock Exchange
To Luxembourg Capital

Anchored at the Luxembourg Stock Exchange and integrated into Luxembourg Venture Days 2026, the event connects Romania's most liquid and visible issuers with an institutional audience that manages capital across private equity, infrastructure, family office, and UCITS/AIF structures.

The programme combines company presentations, sector panels, and one-to-one matchmaking sessions designed to enable substantive investment dialogue.

Why Luxembourg

The natural gateway to European institutional capital.

The world's second-largest fund domicile with €5.7 trillion in assets under management across 3,900+ fund structures.

01

Fund Industry Scale

€5.7 trillion AUM across 3,900+ structures. Largest fund domicile in Europe, second globally.

02

EIB & EIF Headquarters

The EU's primary long-term financing institutions are based here — the natural origination point for EU-backed capital.

03

Private Equity Density

300+ PE and VC managers with significant exposure to infrastructure, buyout, and growth equity across CEE.

04

Family Office Community

Growing network of single- and multi-family offices with increasing allocation to EU convergence markets.

05

UCITS & AIF Ecosystem

Global centre for cross-border fund distribution — UCITS across 70+ jurisdictions and full alternative spectrum.

06

Sovereign & Institutional Access

Direct connections with sovereign wealth vehicles, pension funds, and supranational investors.

  • Macro Fundamentals

    Among the highest GDP growth rates in the EU over the past decade, supported by EU cohesion fund deployment and private consumption growth.

  • EU Convergence Trajectory

    Deepening regulatory alignment with Western European standards, compressing the perceived risk premium for institutional investors.

  • Capital Market Depth

    BVB upgraded to Emerging Market status by FTSE Russell in 2024, anchoring Romania within the institutional index universe.

  • Dividend-Paying Blue-Chips

    Large-cap listed companies in energy, utilities, and banking maintain consistent dividend policies with compelling yield profiles.
